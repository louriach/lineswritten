The portfolio

I don't have a portfolio.

The reality is that every piece of design work I've created has either been lost to the mercy of company failure, or been overwritten by our thirst for redesign.

Working style

I'm someone who attaches a lot of self worth to my work, and tend to overachieve because if I'm engaged, I will do more reps. My last 4 performance reviews have been 4 or 5 out of 5.

Working with other high achievers makes me happy.

I work best in short bursts of extreme efficiency. This might result in 1000 words being written in 30 minutes, or a few dozen screens being pulled together in a few hours.

I don't believe that endless streches of uninterupted time create better work. Constraints, particularly time-based, are a forcing function for decisions to be made. Always move forward.

Before Figma

I've worked in a lot of different industries: e-commerce, charity, hospitality, and travel.

Notably, I've previous worked mostly as a solo designer, or in product teams smaller than 10 people. This comes with it a requirement to be more than a designer, which I think is a good thing.

Cross-departmental collaboration is the best way to reduce silos and improve company culture.
